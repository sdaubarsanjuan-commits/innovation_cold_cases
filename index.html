Mission briefing • Read together

Newer isn't always better.

History is full of brilliant technologies that vanished — lost, abandoned, or replaced by “modern” alternatives that were actually worse in important ways. Your team of investigators will reopen one of these cold cases, figure out why the innovation disappeared, and argue whether the world should bring it back.

Your teamWork in a group of 3–4. Pick a Lead Investigator (watches the clock), an Archivist (digs through the sources), and one or two Advocates (build the argument).
Your jobBuild a 60-second “Case for Re-adoption” pitch and deliver it to the class: how could your lost invention solve a problem we have today?
The rules of evidenceEvery claim in your pitch must come from the case file or its linked sources. No vibes — only evidence.
The twistBe honest about the catch. Every lost technology disappeared for a reason. A strong case names that reason — and answers it.
